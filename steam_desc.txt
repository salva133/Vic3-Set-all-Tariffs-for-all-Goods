[h1]Set all Tariffs for all Goods[/h1]

[b]A quality-of-life mod for Victoria 3[/b] that lets market leaders quickly
bulk-set tariff levels across every good in their market.

[h2]Overview[/h2]
This mod adds a decision called [b]"Set Tariffs"[/b] which triggers an event
chain. As the leader of a market (i.e. you own a world market hub), you can
instantly set import and export tariff levels for [b]all vanilla goods[/b] at
once.

No more clicking through every individual good in the market interface!

[h2]Features[/h2]
[list]
[*]Set tariffs for [b]all goods[/b]
[*]Four tariff levels:
  [*]No tariffs or subventions (zero)
  [*]Low tariffs
  [*]High tariffs
  [*]Maximum tariffs
[*]2-month cooldown after each use
[*]Clean, lightweight event interface with tooltips
[/list]

[h2]How to Use[/h2]
[list]
[*]Play as a country that leads a market.
[*]Go to your [b]Decisions[/b] panel.
[*]Select [b]"Set Tariffs"[/b].
[*]In the next event, pick your desired tariff level.
[/list]

[h2]Compatibility[/h2]
[list]
[*]Only affects vanilla goods
[*]Should be compatible with most other mods (as long as they don't completely
overhaul the goods or tariff system)
[/list]

[h2]Note from the Author[/h2]
You can set tariffs ranging from maximum protectionism down to completely free
trade. Anything beyond that (e.g. subsidizing both imports [i]and[/i] exports
of the same good) makes very little economic sense. I mean, why on earth would
you do that? Do you look like Panama or something?
